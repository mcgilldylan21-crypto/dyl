Why I built HabitIQ

I've always struggled to keep up with habits I wanted to build — like reading every night or drinking more water. I'd start strong, then forget for a few days, and eventually give up completely.

I realized the problem wasn't willpower — it was having no smart system to keep me on track. I wanted something that would remind me at the right time, celebrate my progress, and help me see when I was slipping.

"The goal wasn't just to build an app — it was to solve a real problem I face every day."

HabitIQ started as a sketch on paper. I mapped out what a user would need: a simple dashboard, smart reminders, a streak tracker, and weekly insights. Then I designed each screen to be as clear and frictionless as possible.

Building this project taught me how to think from a user's perspective, how AI can make apps smarter, and how good design can actually change behavior.
